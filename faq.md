## üåç General Questions

### What is MediGlobe?

MediGlobe is an iOS app that helps travelers and international residents find medication equivalents in different countries. It provides brand names, active ingredients, and basic information about medications available worldwide.

### Is MediGlobe free?

Yes! MediGlobe is completely free to download and use with no in-app purchases, subscriptions, or ads.

### Who is MediGlobe for?

MediGlobe is designed for:
- International travelers
- Expatriates living abroad
- Medical tourists
- Digital nomads
- Healthcare professionals working internationally
- Anyone needing medication information in foreign countries

### What countries are supported?

MediGlobe currently covers 50+ countries including:
- All major North American countries (USA, Canada, Mexico)
- Most European countries (UK, Germany, France, Spain, Italy, etc.)
- Major Asian markets (Japan, Australia, South Korea, Singapore, India)
- Latin American countries (Brazil, Argentina, Chile)
- Middle Eastern countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Israel)

New countries are added regularly based on user requests.

### How many medications are in the database?

- Pain relievers and anti-inflammatories
- Antibiotics
- Cardiovascular medications
- Gastrointestinal drugs
- Respiratory medications
- Mental health medications

- Eye and ear drops

### Can I request a medication to be added?

Absolutely! Email us at [mediglobeapp@gmail.com](mailto:mediglobeapp@gmail.com) with:
- The medication name (generic preferred)
- Which country you need it in
- Why it's important to you

We prioritize additions based on user requests.

### Why can't I find my medication?

Possible reasons:
1. **Not in our database yet** - Request it!
2. **Try the generic name** - Search "ibuprofen" instead of "Advil"
3. **Alternative spelling** - Try "paracetamol" vs "acetaminophen"
4. **Not available in that country** - Some medications are country-specific
5. **Offline mode** - Reconnect to internet for full database

### Are the brand names accurate?

We strive for accuracy using trusted sources (RxNorm, OpenFDA, pharmacopeia data), but:
- Brand names can vary by region within countries
- New brands are constantly introduced
- Some brands may be discontinued

**Always verify with a local pharmacist before purchasing.**

### What if the brand name I see isn't available locally?

Brand availability can vary by:
- City or region
- Pharmacy chain
- Recent market changes
- Import restrictions

Try:
1. Asking the pharmacist for alternatives with the same active ingredient
2. Checking another pharmacy
3. Showing them the generic name

## üì± Technical Questions

### What iOS version do I need?

MediGlobe requires iOS 16.0 or later.

Compatible with:
- iPhone (6s and newer)
- iPad (5th generation and newer)
- iPod touch (7th generation)

### Does MediGlobe work offline?

Yes! MediGlobe offers offline functionality:

**Works Offline:**
- Search local database (150+ medications)
- View favorites
- Access previously searched medications (cached)
- Basic medication information

**Requires Internet:**
- Searching new medications not in local database
- Updating country information
- Location-based country detection
- Latest brand name updates

### Why is the app slow?

Possible causes:
1. **Slow internet connection** - Try offline mode or wait
2. **API rate limiting** - Wait a minute and try again
3. **Large search results** - Normal for common medications
4. **Device storage low** - Free up space on your device
5. **Outdated iOS** - Update to latest iOS version

See our [Troubleshooting Guide](troubleshooting.md) for solutions.

### How much storage does MediGlobe use?

- **App Size**: ~15-25 MB
- **Local Database**: ~5-10 MB
- **Cached Searches**: Varies (typically <50 MB)

Total: Usually under 100 MB

### Can I use MediGlobe on iPad?

Yes! MediGlobe works on iPad with full functionality. The interface adapts to the larger screen.

### Is there an Android version?

Not yet. MediGlobe is currently iOS-only. An Android version may be developed based on user demand.

## üåê Travel Questions

### Can I use MediGlobe abroad?

Yes! That's exactly what it's designed for.

**Before Travel:**
1. Search your regular medications
2. Save them to favorites
3. Note the local brand names
4. Take screenshots for offline reference

**During Travel:**
1. Use offline mode if needed
2. Show the app to pharmacists
3. Verify with local healthcare providers
4. Keep prescriptions handy

### Do I need a prescription for medications abroad?

**It depends:**
- **Prescription requirements vary by country**
- Some over-the-counter drugs in one country require prescriptions elsewhere
- Some countries have stricter controls
- Always carry your original prescription

**MediGlobe shows medication information but cannot determine if a prescription is needed.**

### Can I bring medications across borders?

**Varies by country and medication:**
- Most allow personal-use quantities
- Some countries ban certain medications
- Prescription documentation may be required
- Controlled substances have strict rules

**Check with:**
- Your destination country's customs
- Your airline
- Your embassy or consulate
- The medication's manufacturer

MediGlobe does not provide customs or import advice.

### What if my medication isn't available in my destination?

Options:
1. **Bring enough from home** (check customs limits)
2. **Ask your doctor about alternatives** before traveling
3. **Consult local doctors** for local prescriptions
4. **Contact your embassy** for medical assistance resources

### Should I bring my prescriptions when traveling?

**Absolutely! Always bring:**
- Original prescriptions
- Medication in original packaging
- Doctor's letter explaining the medication
- Generic name of the medication
- Enough supply for your trip (+extra)

This helps with:
- Customs and border control
- Replacing lost medications
- Communicating with foreign healthcare providers
- Emergency medical care
